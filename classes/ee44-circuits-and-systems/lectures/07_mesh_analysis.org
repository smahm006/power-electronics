* Key Concepts
** Mesh Analysis
It's the other side of the coin of nodal analysis. Instead of KCL's at nodes we calculate KVL's at meshes.

Weaker than nodal analysis because due to circuit topology, you may not be able to find a mesh that covers certain elements.

* Worked Examples
** Example: 1
*Given:*
[[../figures/lecture7_example1_figure1.png]]
*Find: Mesh Analysis of circuit
*Solution:*
*** 1. Choose a current convention
Sir Ali chose the current going clockwise, and assumed voltage is the potential difference from + to -.
*** 2. Derive KVL for all meshes
\begin{align*}
-V_1 + R_1 i_1 - R_2(i_1 - i_2) = 0 \\
V_2 + - R_2(i_1 - i_2) + R_3 i_2 = 0
\end{align*}
*** 3. Re-write in matrix form
\begin{pmatrix}
R_1 + R_2 & -R_2 \\
-R_2 & R_2 + R_3
\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}
i_1 \\ i_2
\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}
V_1 \\ V_2
\end{pmatrix}
*** 4. solve the matrix
to solve our matrix
\begin{equation*}
\tilde{Z} \; \vec{i} = \vec{V_{s}}
\implies \vec{i} = \vec{V_{s}} \; \tilde{Z^{-1}} \;
\text{where $Z_s$ = stimuli voltage}
\end{equation*}
this type of matrix is called z-matrix. z is a symbol for impedence.
